HIV Among Adolescents: A Global Health Crisis

Introduction

HIV/AIDS It remains a major global health challenge and affects most vulnerable populations at the world, especially adolescents aged 10 to 19 years. It examines UNICEF data on HIV infections of adolescents world wide, and focuses on trends, geographic distributions and correlated factors.

Our analysis reveals several critical insights:

  1. HIV localization: While HIV infections among adolescents are not evenly distributed globally, sub-Saharan African countries bear disproportional amount of burden.

  2. Gender Disparities: The fact that most affected regions have higher rates of infection among female adolescents than males indicates vulnerabilities among females that differ from males.

  3. Across Countries : Increase in adolescent HIV infection is associated with lower life expectancy and GDP per capita, indicating that there is a correlation between economic development and public health outcomes.

  4. Temporal Trends: However, global efforts have reduced global incidence of new HIV infections effectively, while such efforts have been uneven across regions and among demographic groups.

#| label: data-cleaning

# Clean the HIV infection data
# Convert '<100' values to a numeric representation (e.g., 50)
indicator_df['obs_value_numeric'] = indicator_df['obs_value'].replace('<100', '50')
indicator_df['obs_value_numeric'] = pd.to_numeric(indicator_df['obs_value_numeric'], errors='coerce')

# Filter for relevant age group and recent years
hiv_data = indicator_df[indicator_df['current_age'] == '10 to 19 years old'].copy()

# Create a more recent year filter (e.g., last 10 years in the data)
recent_years = sorted(hiv_data['time_period'].unique())[-10:]
recent_hiv_data = hiv_data[hiv_data['time_period'].isin(recent_years)]

# Aggregate data by country and sex
country_sex_hiv = recent_hiv_data.groupby(['country', 'sex'])['obs_value_numeric'].sum().reset_index()

# Get the most recent year for each country in the metadata
metadata_recent = metadata_df.sort_values('year', ascending=False).drop_duplicates('country')

# Merge HIV data with country metadata
merged_data = pd.merge(country_sex_hiv, metadata_recent, on='country', how='inner')

# Prepare data for a world map
world = gpd.read_file('https://naciscdn.org/naturalearth/110m/cultural/ne_110m_admin_0_countries.zip')
world = world.rename(columns={'ADM0_A3': 'alpha_3_code'})

# Create a mapping dataframe for the map visualization
map_data = recent_hiv_data.groupby('country')['obs_value_numeric'].sum().reset_index()
map_data = pd.merge(map_data, metadata_recent[['country', 'alpha_3_code']], on='country', how='inner')
map_data = pd.merge(world, map_data, on='alpha_3_code', how='right')

# Prepare time series data
time_series_data = hiv_data.groupby(['time_period', 'sex'])['obs_value_numeric'].sum().reset_index()
time_series_data['time_period'] = pd.to_numeric(time_series_data['time_period'])

# Prepare data for scatterplot (Life expectancy vs. HIV infections)
scatter_data = country_sex_hiv.groupby('country')['obs_value_numeric'].sum().reset_index()
scatter_data = pd.merge(scatter_data,
                        metadata_recent[['country', 'Life expectancy at birth, total (years)', 'GDP per capita (constant 2015 US$)']],
                        on='country', how='inner')
scatter_data = scatter_data.rename(columns={'Life expectancy at birth, total (years)': 'life_expectancy',
                                           'GDP per capita (constant 2015 US$)': 'gdp_per_capita'})

Analysis of World Map:

HIV infections among adolescents in the world were frankly revealed by the world map visualization. We can clearly observe that:

  • Sub-Saharan Africa has the highest burden of infection; countries such as South Africa, Nigeria, Kenya and Tanzania have much higher levels of infection. Particularly, Southern Africa is a hotspot with the highest rates globally in some regions.

These statistics reveal a geographic concentration that implies what is happening in certain areas in the world, if intervention were targeted there, would have a large effect on reducing the global incidence of HIV among adolescents.

The bar chart comparing HIV infections by gender in the top 15 most affected countries reveals critical patterns:

Gender disparity: Females are not less infected than males but often better than males in most high burden country. This suggests vulnerable points at the gender level that need targeting interventions.

Regional concentration: The world map indicates that the majority of the highest burden countries are concentrated in sub Saharan Africa which corroborate with the common patterns noted.

Regional Analysis

Sub-Saharan Africa

The HIV epidemic among adolescents is far from over in sub-Saharan Africa. South Africa, Nigeria, and Kenya, among other countries, provide the highest number of new yearly infections. Several factors contribute to this:

  • Limited access to healthcare services
  • Socioeconomic challenges affecting education and prevention programs
  • Cultural factors influencing sexual health education
  • Gaps in prevention of mother-to-child transmission programs

Asia and the Pacific

Although the numbers are slightly fewer than Africa, many Asian countries have rising need for caring for adolescents infected with HIV, especially among those that are vulnerable, such as:

  • Young people who inject drugs
  • Young men who have sex with men
  • Adolescents engaged in sex work

Other Regions

HIV infection among Adolescents varies, with patterns of infection in Latin America, Eastern Europe and the Middle East varying according to populations that are key affected by certain factors such as:

  • Migration patterns
  • Access to sexual health education
  • Stigma and discrimination
  • Healthcare system capacity
